2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. Cookies are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, remember preferences, and provide information to website owners.

Session cookies are temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser.

Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them.

First-party cookies are set by the website you are visiting.

Third-party cookies are set by a domain other than the one you are visiting.

Similar technologies may include pixels, tags, local storage, analytics scripts, call tracking identifiers, or session identifiers.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

Essential cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They may support security, network management, accessibility, form behavior, session stability, and cookie consent preferences. You cannot opt out of cookies that are strictly necessary for basic site operation.

Cookie consent: remembers whether you accepted, declined, or customized cookie preferences.

Session management: may maintain basic site or form behavior while browsing.

3.2 Analytics Cookies

Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information such as page views, traffic sources, general interaction patterns, device information, and performance data. This helps us improve the website and understand which service pages are useful.

3.3 Functional Cookies

Functional cookies may enable enhanced functionality, remember preferences, or temporarily support form interactions. For example, they may help remember cookie settings or improve the experience when you return.

3.4 Marketing or Measurement Cookies

Marketing or measurement cookies may help evaluate advertising performance, understand whether an inquiry came from a campaign, and measure engagement with contact forms, phone links, or service pages. We use these technologies only where permitted by law and consistent with your choices.

5. How to Manage Cookies

You have the right to accept or reject non-essential cookies. Our cookie banner allows you to accept, decline, or manage analytics preferences. Your selection may be stored in local storage under a cookie preference key so that the banner does not reappear on every page.

5.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can modify your browser settings to block, delete, or limit cookies. Browser controls vary, so review the help resources for your browser, such as Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, Safari, or Microsoft Edge.

5.2 Opt-Out Tools

You may also be able to opt out of certain third-party analytics or advertising cookies through tools offered by those third parties or industry groups, such as analytics opt-out tools, the Digital Advertising Alliance, or the Network Advertising Initiative.

Blocking or deleting cookies may affect your experience on our website. Some features, analytics, form behavior, or user preferences may not function properly if cookies are disabled.
