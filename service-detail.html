Service guide

The basic path from first concern to a cleaner, drier plan.

This page explains the shared logic across inspection, cleanup, emergency response, HVAC odor support, commercial needs, and prevention planning.

Framework

First conversation Assessment Work phases Prevention Service groups
First conversation

Every useful mold request starts with context.

A helpful first conversation should cover property type, location, affected area, visible signs, odor patterns, leak history, urgency, access limits, occupancy concerns, and whether the customer already has testing, photos, or insurance involvement.

Assessment

Assessment separates symptom from source.

Independent local professionals may evaluate moisture, building materials, ventilation, HVAC pathways, drainage, and hidden cavities. Testing may be recommended when it helps answer a specific question or document a condition.

Work phases
01

Source control

Address water intrusion, leaks, condensation, or humidity drivers before relying on cleaning alone.

02

Containment

Separate affected work areas and use filtration when disturbance could spread dust or debris.

03

Removal

Remove unsalvageable porous materials and clean remaining structural surfaces according to the agreed work plan.

04

Closeout

Discuss completion notes, verification options, prevention steps, and maintenance recommendations.

Prevention

Prevention is the difference between cleanup and durable improvement.

Long-term protection can involve dehumidification, ventilation correction, grading, gutters, vapor barriers, insulation, plumbing repair, roof repair, HVAC service, or routine monitoring.
